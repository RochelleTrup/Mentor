Questionaire

Introduction

It is always hard to find the time to focus on yourself, but I promise, once you start, you’ll be engaged and if you live by the code, you will have a fulfilled life, it will either validate you’re doing it right or it will give you the confidence to make changes.

It’s about understanding what makes you happy and then making sure you’re prioritizing the correct things. I generally come up detail 5-15 bullet points per question, I have given some examples for some questions to get you started.

Summary and Outcome

  1. Paradigm shift for the year - If you could make one change in the way you behave or in your own habits that would be transformative, what would that be?
  2. 3 personal guidelines - These are 3 pieces of advice you want to give yourself to shape the way you behave next year.
    These are not goals but personal advice. An example would be "Pause before speaking" or "don't interrupt people"
  3. List any long-term goals that you have. These will be 5–10-year goals. An example might be "Move to a smaller house or apartment"
  4. 2 things your partner wants you to do or change next year. You may have to ask him/her
  5. What is the most important role you have this year? This will change from year to year, but this is the role that you want to put most effort into
  6. For every role make a list of goals. Some will have only a few, some might have a lot more. This is your long list of goals
    • Self - Mental, physical, spiritual, humanist (interest in human race)
    • Mother
    • Wife
    • Daughter/Daughter in law
    • Friend
    • Comapany Director
    • Trustee
    • NED
    • Charity Worker/Mentor
  7. Choose 10 of your long list of goals that will make the most difference in your life. These will be your goals. For each of your 10 goals, write a note to yourself
    as to why it’s so important. If you can't think of a reason, you should consider substituting another goal which has a more compelling impact on you.
  8. Write your annual treat plan? This is your monthly plan of holidays and other "treats" you will look forward to through the year
  9. General bucket List, this doesn’t change but you add to it annually
    • UK - Cornwall, Devon, Wales, Ireland, Highlands, Skye
    • Australia, New Zealand and the South Pacific Islands
    • Hawaii
    • DD landings and beaches of Normandy
  10. What habits do you need to change to meet your goals and personal guidelines?
  11. Choose one of your goals to be your "Wildly important goal". If you do nothing else this year, you will do this one
  12. Finally make one note with your paradigm shift, key role, 3 personal guidelines and 10 goals with reasons. Highlight your wildly important goal
